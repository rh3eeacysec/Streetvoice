Frequently Asked Questions

Is StreetCredits real money, or redeemable for anything? ⌄

StreetCredits is a genuinely working point and leaderboard system — your activity is tracked for real. But it currently has no monetary value and isn't redeemable for anything from a government body or third party. It exists to recognize and encourage civic participation, not as a payment or rewards-redemption system.

Do I need to create an account or log in? ⌄

No. You're automatically given an anonymous identity the moment you open the app — no email, password, or signup form required. Your reports and credits are tied to that anonymous session.

What languages does voice reporting actually support? ⌄

English and Hindi, selected via a toggle before you speak. Browser voice recognition technology can't reliably auto-detect which language you're speaking, so you pick first — the AI still cleans up and understands mixed Hinglish speech either way.

Why doesn't the voice button work in my browser? ⌄

Voice input relies on a browser feature currently supported in Chrome and Edge, but not Firefox or Safari. If you're on an unsupported browser, the button will say so directly rather than failing silently — you can still type your report manually.

Are the awareness posters real AI-generated images? ⌄

The poster's headline, color palette, severity rating, and hashtag are generated by a real Gemini AI call. The visual layout itself is a designed template, not AI-generated artwork — we're being upfront about that distinction.

Is my reported data public, and is it secure? ⌄

Reports are intentionally public — that's what makes the map and community verification useful. Access is gated behind real authentication (you can't read or write data without a valid session), and you can only modify your own profile and your own reports, not anyone else's.
